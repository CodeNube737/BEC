===============================================================================
  POWER SUPPLY NOTES
===============================================================================

1. Motor Power: 6-12V DC (depends on motor specifications)
   - Check motor voltage rating
   - Ensure current capacity > total motor draw
   - Typical: 2A per motor × 4 motors = 8A minimum recommended

2. Arduino Power:
   - Option A: USB cable from computer (programming/testing only)
   - Option B: External 7-12V to VIN pin (when mobile)
   - Option C: 5V to 5V pin (if H-bridge has 5V regulator output)

3. Common Ground: CRITICAL - All grounds must be connected together
   - Arduino GND
   - H-bridge Logic GND
   - H-bridge Motor Power GND
   - Motor Power Supply GND

  PIN SUMMARY
===============================================================================

Arduino Pin | Function              | Connects To
------------+-----------------------+-----------------------------------
3           | Input - Backward      | Switch to GND
4           | Input - Forward       | Switch to GND
5           | PWM - Right Motor     | H-bridge ENB
6           | Input - Left Turn     | Switch to GND
7           | Input - Right Turn    | Switch to GND
8           | Left Motor Dir 1      | H-bridge IN1
9           | Left Motor Dir 2      | H-bridge IN2
10          | PWM - Left Motor      | H-bridge ENA
11          | Right Motor Dir 1     | H-bridge IN3
12          | Right Motor Dir 2     | H-bridge IN4
GND         | Ground                | H-bridge GND, Power Supply GND

===============================================================================
  TESTING SEQUENCE
===============================================================================

1. Connect Arduino to computer via USB (NO motors yet)
2. Upload motor_controller.ino
3. Open Serial Monitor (9600 baud)
4. Verify "Motor Controller Initialized" message
5. Test each switch input - verify Serial Monitor shows direction changes
6. Disconnect USB
7. Connect H-bridge to Arduino (still NO motors)
8. Power Arduino + H-bridge
9. Measure voltages at H-bridge outputs with multimeter
10. Connect motors
11. Test each direction with wheels off ground
12. Test full operation with robot on ground
